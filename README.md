# Multispectral Sensors in Remote Sensing

This repository is a coursework / academic project from the **Department of Mining Engineering** that surveys the major **multispectral sensors used in Earth observation** — covering their evolution, working principles, technical specifications, and applications. It includes a presentation deck and an accompanying **Multispectral Sensors Database** that consolidates the key parameters of each sensor for quick reference.

## Project Overview

Multispectral sensors are at the core of modern Earth observation, supplying the imagery used in land-cover mapping, agriculture, mining and mineral exploration, water-resource studies, urban planning, and disaster management. This project walks through how multispectral remote sensing has developed from the early Landsat era to today's high-resolution commercial and constellation-based missions, and compares the major sensors that have shaped the field.

The materials are pitched at a general technical audience — remote-sensing students, GIS practitioners, and professionals in earth sciences, mining/geology, agriculture, and environmental studies who want a consolidated reference on multispectral sensors.

## Repository Contents

- **Multispectral Sensors in RS_choladeck.pdf** — A presentation deck (slides) prepared as a coursework group project. It introduces the basics of multispectral remote sensing, walks through major sensors developed to date, and summarises their specifications and applications.
- **Multispectral_Sensors_Database (3).xlsx** — The **Multispectral Sensors Database**: a structured spreadsheet that catalogues multispectral sensors and their key parameters (host platform, operating agency, mission timeline, number of bands, spectral range, spatial/temporal/radiometric resolution, swath width, and primary applications).

## What the Presentation Covers

The deck is built around three broad themes — fundamentals, individual sensor profiles, and a consolidated tabular summary.

### 1. Fundamentals of multispectral remote sensing

- **Scanning mechanisms:** whiskbroom (across-track) and pushbroom (along-track) imaging.
- **Key parameters:** spatial resolution, spectral resolution, and temporal (revisit) resolution.
- **Spectral reflectance curves** for typical surfaces (water, soil, green vegetation) across the visible to mid-infrared range.

### 2. Sensor profiles

For each sensor, the deck typically lists the host platform, operating agency, launch year / mission timeline, sensor type, orbit, altitude, swath width, spatial/spectral/temporal/radiometric resolution, and major applications. Sensors covered include:

- **MSS — Multispectral Scanner** (Landsat 1–5; NASA/USGS, USA).
- **TM — Thematic Mapper** (Landsat 4–5; NASA/USGS, USA).
- **ETM+ — Enhanced Thematic Mapper Plus** (Landsat 7; NASA/USGS, USA).
- **OLI — Operational Land Imager** (Landsat 8 & 9; NASA/USGS, USA).
- **HRV — High Resolution Visible** (SPOT series, France/CNES).
- **NAOMI — New AstroSat Optical Modular Imager** (ALOS-3, Japan/JAXA, 2024) — successor to AVNIR-2.
- **MODIS — Moderate Resolution Imaging Spectroradiometer** (Terra/Aqua, NASA).
- **ASTER — Advanced Spaceborne Thermal Emission and Reflection Radiometer** (Terra, NASA, 1999).
- **AVHRR — Advanced Very High Resolution Radiometer** (NOAA polar-orbiting satellites, operational since 1978).
- **VIIRS — Visible Infrared Imaging Radiometer Suite** (Suomi NPP, NOAA-20, NOAA-21; NASA & NOAA, USA).
- **MERIS — Medium Resolution Imaging Spectrometer** (Envisat, ESA).
- **QuickBird** (USA, 2001–2015).
- **WorldView series** — including high-resolution platforms such as WorldView-4 (USA, 2019).
- **MSI — Multispectral Imager** (Sentinel-2A & Sentinel-2B, ESA Copernicus).
- **AVNIR-2 — Advanced Visible and Near Infrared Radiometer Type-2** (ALOS/Daichi, JAXA, 2006–2011).
- **LISS — Linear Imaging and Self-Scanning Sensor**, LISS-I to LISS-IV (IRS-1A/1B/1C/1D and Resourcesat series, ISRO, India).
- **AEISS — Arirang Electro-Optical Imaging Subsystem** (KOMPSAT-2 / 3 / 3A, KARI, South Korea).
- **SkySat Camera** (SkySat constellation, USA, 2013–present).
- **RSI — Remote Sensing Instrument** (FORMOSAT-2, Taiwan, 2004–2016).
- **VMI — Vegetation Monitoring Instrument** (SPOT-4 and SPOT-5, France).

### 3. Consolidated specification tables

Toward the end, the deck includes consolidated tables that present each sensor's host vehicle, mission years, number of bands, spatial/spectral/temporal/radiometric resolution, wavelength range, and swath width side-by-side for easy comparison.

## About the Multispectral Sensors Database

The accompanying **Multispectral Sensors Database** is intended as a quick-reference table to support the presentation. For each sensor it captures the kind of information needed to compare missions side-by-side, including:

- Sensor / instrument name and host platform or satellite.
- Operating agency or organisation and country.
- Launch year and operational status (active / decommissioned).
- Number of spectral bands and their general spectral range.
- Spatial resolution (multispectral and panchromatic where applicable).
- Swath width and revisit period.
- Radiometric resolution.
- Primary application domains.

The database is shared in `.xlsx` format so that it can be sorted, filtered, and extended easily.

## Applications Highlighted in the Deck

Across the sensor profiles, the deck highlights typical multispectral remote-sensing applications such as:

- Land-use / land-cover mapping and change detection.
- Vegetation monitoring, crop health, and yield estimation (e.g., NDVI/EVI products).
- Forestry — biomass, canopy structure, and deforestation monitoring.
- Geological and mineral exploration (e.g., hydrothermal alteration mapping with SWIR/TIR bands).
- Coastal-zone and water-resource studies, including bathymetry and water-quality monitoring.
- Ocean colour, chlorophyll, and sea-surface temperature products.
- Urban planning, infrastructure mapping, and disaster management (floods, fires, earthquakes, landslides).
- Climate and atmospheric studies (aerosols, clouds, land-surface temperature).

1. Open **Multispectral Sensors in RS_choladeck.pdf** for the conceptual overview, individual sensor profiles, and applications.
