# Multispectral Sensors in Remote Sensing


This repository is a coursework / academic project that surveys the major **multispectral sensors used in Earth observation** — covering their evolution, working principles, technical specifications, and applications. It includes a presentation deck and an accompanying **Multispectral Sensors Database** that consolidates the key parameters of each sensor for quick reference.


## Project Overview


Multispectral sensors are at the core of modern Earth observation, supplying the imagery used in land-cover mapping, agriculture, mining and mineral exploration, water-resource studies, urban planning, and disaster management. This project walks through how multispectral remote sensing has developed from the early Landsat era to today's high-resolution commercial and constellation-based missions, and compares the major sensors that have shaped the field.


The materials are pitched at a general technical audience — remote-sensing students, GIS practitioners, and professionals in earth sciences, mining/geology, agriculture, and environmental studies who want a consolidated reference on multispectral sensors.


## Repository Contents


- **Multispectral Sensors in RS.pdf** — A presentation deck (slides) prepared as a coursework group project. It introduces the basics of multispectral remote sensing, walks through major sensors developed to date, and summarises their specifications and applications.
- **Multispectral_Sensors_Database (3).xlsx** — The **Multispectral Sensors Database**: a structured spreadsheet that catalogues multispectral sensors and their key parameters (host platform, operating agency, mission timeline, number of bands, spectral range, spatial/temporal/radiometric resolution, swath width, and primary applications).


## What the Presentation Covers


The deck is built around three broad themes — fundamentals, individual sensor profiles, and a consolidated tabular summary.


### 1. Fundamentals of multispectral remote sensing


- **Scanning mechanisms:** whiskbroom (across-track) and pushbroom (along-track) imaging.
- **Key parameters:** spatial resolution, spectral resolution, and temporal (revisit) resolution.
- **Spectral reflectance curves** for typical surfaces (water, soil, green vegetation) across the visible to mid-infrared range.
